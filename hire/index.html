Previous gigs

For the past 3 years, I've been the lead iOS app developer at Parts Town in Addison, Illinois. During that time, we've added feature after feature to our e-commerce and reference app, like interactive wire diagrams, smart manuals, and order history. In addition to writing the code for all these projects, I also took the helm on the UI and UX tasks. In fact, most of the icons in the Parts Town app were designed by me.

While at Parts Town, we also piloted a totally custom app for First Choice Catering Spares, our parts distribution subsidiary in the UK (the project was later handed off once they hired a rockstar in-house development team, so I can't take all the credit for the outstanding app you see today).

Before Parts Town, I worked at RR Donnelley in Elgin IL. In my role there, I built lots of software tools to streamline team's workflow. Those tools included Mac desktop applications (written with Objective-C and AppKit), web applications, and server-side scripts for automation. These tools had to interact with our enterprise asset management systems. In hindsight, the team had accidently self-organized into something that sort of resembled an agile development team, with software updates being rolled out regularly (sometimes multiple times a week), and developers interacting directly with end-users to identify and resolve pain points.

What I'm looking for in my ideal role

  • Work I not only take pride in, but also get excited about. I want to tweet and blog about every cool new feature we launch!
  • An employer that is transparent with both their customers and their employees.
  • Projects that focus on value for the end-user, not corporate flattery.
  • Healthy work-life balance. I'll happily stay late or work weekends if it means fixing critical issues that directly affect the customer, but those incidents should few and far between.
  • Casual dress code. I want to work for a business that values its people more than its corporate image.
  • An epmployer that values an employee's input as much as their output. It's hard to take pride in your work when someone else calls the shots and takes all credit. Inviting the engineering team to be part of the decision making process gives them a sense of ownership of the project, which results in better code.
  • A mostly quiet workspace with some amount of privicy. I'm okay with cubicles, but I'd like to avoid open-plan offices if possible.
  • Minimal travel. I get travel anxiety, plus I'm a tall dude which makes air travel especially unpleasant. If your role requires more than 2 trips per year, I don't think I'll be a good fit.
  • Remote work or comfortable commute from Elgin, IL (far west suburbs of Chicago). Schaumburg and Downers Grove are both good locations, but I'm afraid I'm not looking to commute into the city of Chicago.

If the position you're hiring for doesn't meet every one of these criteria, that's okay! Maybe we can still work something out.
